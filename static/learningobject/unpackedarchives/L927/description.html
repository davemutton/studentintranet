Start: Down to Earth: metals matter
 
Title: Down to Earth: metals matter 
Description: Examine everyday things around a house to find out which metals they contain. For example, the inner part of a microwave oven contains aluminium, copper, iron and manganese. Investigate the properties of each metal to explain why they are suited to particular uses. Work out how much of each metal is needed to make a particular product. Calculate supplies of resources needed to meet global demand. This learning object is one in a series of three objects 
Topics: Household appliances; Industrial design; Metals; Nonrenewable resources; Ore deposits; Periodic table; Recycling; Supply and demand 
Keywords: Commodities, Household appliances, Industrial design, Metalloids, Metals, Nonrenewable resources, Ore deposits, Periodic table, Recycling, Supply and demand 
Learning area: Science 
Strand: Natural and processed materials; Science inquiry skills; Science understanding 
Concept: Industrial applications; Materials; Mineralogy; Natural resources; Periodic table; Physical properties; Quantitative data 
Audience user level: 5; 6; 7; 8  Audience type: Student 
Age range: 9-14  Audience sector: School 
Key learning objectives:
  • Students identify metals commonly found in household objects.
  • Students relate demand for commodities to supply of mineral resources.
  • Students relate properties of metals and metalloids to their industrial applications.
Educational value:
  • Demonstrates that growing world demand for commodities is dependent on limited supplies of mineral resources.
  • Illustrates the composition of metallic and metalloid components within household commodities.
  • Emphasises that alloys and composite materials are commonly used in the manufacture of household commodities.
  • Links the periodic table to descriptions of the properties and uses of metals and metalloids.
  • Provides an interactive notebook to record and print facts about commodities, metals and metalloids.
  • Provides an interactive calculator and global map to work out relationships between world demand for commodities and supply of mineral resources.
  • Includes detailed information about recycling metals in Australia.
  • Includes a series of questions which could be used to create a worksheet.
