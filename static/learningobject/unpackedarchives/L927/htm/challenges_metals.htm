Challenges
  1. Different metals have different properties. For one object make a list of the metals found in the object and identify the properties of each the metals that make them suitable for use in this object.

  2. Select 10 different objects in the house that you use regularly and identify the metals they contain. Identify properties of each metal which make them suitable for use in the object. Draw up a table as below to record your information.

    Object Metal Property
    computer gold Ductile – can be made into wires easily
    Conducts electricity well

  3. Select one metallic element. Use ‘Metal Matters’ and other reference materials to find out what this element is used for. Conduct research to determine the source of this element in Australia and the processes that are involved in obtaining supplies of the pure metal. Prepare a report on the importance of this metal to modern society.

  4. Aluminium and Magnesium have similar properties. Identify objects in the house that contain either magnesium or aluminium or both. If aluminium was to significantly increase in price would it be possible to use magnesium in its place in all of these objects? Give reasons to support your answer.

  5. Gold, Silver and Copper are the only metals which are found in a pure form in the Earth. All of the other metals are found as components of minerals. Select six different metals and identify the minerals from which they are extracted and where these minerals are mined in Australia.

  6. It is possible to recycle some metals. Conduct research to find out which household items can be recycled and how the different metals are separated from one another in the recycling process.

  7. Use the internet and other resources to find out the current cost of one kilogram of each metal. Discuss why there is such a great variation in the prices.

  8. Iron is used in many household items. If the price of iron increased significantly what would happen to the price of these items? Suggest alternative metals which could be used in place of iron and discuss the costs and benefits of replacing iron in this way.

  9. Which metal is included in the largest number of household objects? In which mineral is this metal usually found? Where are mines for this mineral located in Australia?

  10. Gold is malleable, ductile, inert, and an excellent conductor of heat and electricity. It is also expensive. If the price of gold doubled people might have to consider using an alternative metal. Select some objects that contain gold. Identify possible alternative, less expensive metals that could be used to replace the gold in these objects. Give reasons for your choice.

  11. The ‘virtual metal detector’ provides information only about the metals that are present in this object. Identify which non-metal elements could also be present and where they could be found.
