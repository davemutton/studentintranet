Start: Sugoi! Let's eat
 
Title: Sugoi! Let's eat 
Description: Explore the Japanese names for drinks, fruits and other foods. Listen to a conversation between a chef and customers placing an order. Listen to a conversation between a boy and a fruit shopkeeper. Listen to the order of phrases for preparing food, cooking and eating. Arrange phrases for a cooking session. This learning object is a combination of three objects in the same series. 
Topics: Conversations; Cooking; Dialogue; Drinks; Food products; Japanese language; Ordinal numbers; Ovaries (Plants); Questions; Shopping; Shops; Vegetables; Vocabulary 
Keywords: Chefs, Conversations, Cooking, Dialogue, Drinks, Food preparation, Food products, Japanese language, Ordinal numbers, Ovaries (Plants), Questions, Shopkeepers, Shopping, Shops, Vegetables, Vocabulary 
Learning area: Languages Other Than English 
Strand: Oral interaction; Reading and responding; Understanding language 
Concept: Conversation; Japanese; Language systems; Questions; Shop phrases; Social situations 
Audience user level: 0; 1; 2; 3; 4; 5; 6; 7; 8  Audience type: Student 
Age range: 4-14  Audience sector: Preschool; School 
Key learning objectives:
  • Students identify the names of drinks, fruits and other foods.
  • Students identify Japanese numbers and food preparation phrases.
Educational value:
  • Students explore the names of drinks, fruits and other foods.
  • Students listen to food and drink orders in Japanese.
  • Students explore the Japanese phrases for stages of food preparation.
  • Students explore how to respond positively and negatively to a request.
  • Students explore the use of ordinal numbers.
  • Students explore the Japanese phrases: 'What would you like to have?', '..., please', (fried noodles, ice-cream, sushi, hamburger, cola, orange juice, water), 'Do you have …?', 'Yes, we have/No, we don't have ...' (pineapples, bananas, apple, pear, strawberries, grapes, watermelon, mandarins).
