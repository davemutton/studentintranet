Display, rotate and resize Platonic solids.


Instructions

A Platonic solid is a polyhedron whose faces are identical regular polygons. The ancient Greeks were able to show that there are exactly five Platonic solids.

Follow the instructions to display, rotate, and resize Platonic solids. You can also select vertices, edges, and faces, and show that the number of vertices, minus the number of edges, plus the number of faces is equal to 2 (Euler’s formula).

Rotate the Platonic solid

Click and drag anywhere in the workspace. The Platonic solid will rotate.

Colour faces of the Platonic solid

  1. Click on one of the colour buttons.
  2. Hold the Shift key down on your keyboard and click on a face of the Platonic solid. The face will be painted the selected colour.

Colour edges or vertices of the Platonic solid

Hold the Shift key down on your keyboard and click on an edge or vertex. Edges will be coloured white; vertices will be coloured black.

Display a different Platonic solid

Click on the New Shape button. A different Platonic solid will be displayed.

Clear counted items

Click on the Reset Shape button. The colour is removed from all faces, edges, and vertices of the Platonic solid.

Show the wire-frame version of the Platonic solid

Click on (check) the Transparent check box. A wire-frame version of the Platonic solid will be displayed.

Change the size of the Platonic solid

Click and drag the vertical slider. The Platonic solid will dynamically resize.
